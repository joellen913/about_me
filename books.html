"BORN A CRIME"   

About The Book

In the novel Born a Crime, author, Late Night Talk Show Host,
and South African Comedian; Trevor Noah goes through his
life as a half-black half-white child in Africa during the apartheid
crisis from 1970 until the early 1990s. As a mixed child during
this time period, Trevor Noah becomes a walking
crime; portraying the interrelations of a white and black
couple which was deemed illegal by the segregation system
of the South African Government. Noah details
his home life, his experience
being excluded from both sides of race,
his times of mischief, and more in this
dramatic, hilarious, and touching novel.

"WHEN YOU REACH ME"   

About The Book

In the book When You Reach Me , author Rebecca Stead,
writes about a protagonist, Miranda Sinclair, and her
experience as she receives a strange note asking her to
record future events and write down the location of her
spare key. As the novel progresses, it dives further into
the depths of time-travel and space, as she
starts receiving more mysterious messages from
somebody who knows all about her, including
things that have not even happened yet.
Each message brings her closer to believing that only
she can prevent a tragic death, and the novel
unravels in the most unexpected of ways.

"BETWEEN THE WORLD AND ME"   

About The Book

In the book Between the World and Me , author Ta-Nehisi
Coates writes a novel written as a letter to the author's
teenage son about the feelings, symbolism, and realities
associated with being Black in the United States. This
novel details a large amount of broad themes, including
race and systemic oppression; fear; father-son relationships;
the search for identity; education; and justice, among
others.
